## 6. Review Disposition Model

The human independent reviewer must select one final disposition:

```text
Sufficiency determination classification confirmed
Changes required
Rejected
Unresolved
```

Sufficiency determination classification confirmed:

All review criteria are Pass, no material contradiction remains, and the exact
Step 295.19 Sufficient classification may proceed only to a separate future
final release-decision authorization checkpoint.

Changes required:

One or more review criteria require bounded correction to the sufficiency
determination record, its application, or an earlier affected governance stage.
No final release-decision authorization may begin.

Rejected:

The sufficiency determination contains a material governance conflict,
non-evidence violation, invalid authority boundary, unresolvable contradiction,
or unsafe evidence boundary. The result must not be used for final
release-decision authorization planning.

Unresolved:

The reviewer cannot safely assess one or more material criteria without
prohibited material or unsupported inference. The result must not be used for
final release-decision authorization planning.

## 7. Human Reviewer Instructions

The independent human reviewer should:

- review the exact Step 295.19 sufficiency-determination decision record
  against all listed criteria;
- avoid inspecting or requesting prohibited raw, sensitive, or private
  material;
- avoid inferring zero findings from Plugin Check command success, silence,
  human-readable output, or private implementation behavior;
- review fixed criteria A through J and the Sufficient outcome only through
  approved safe category-level records;
- record one final disposition using the defined four-category model;
- record only safe category-level reasons and required correction categories;
- avoid making or authorizing a final WordPress.org release decision, public
  release approval, or acceptance prediction.

The reviewer should not record real names, organization names, credentials,
secret values, raw evidence, or private evidence.
